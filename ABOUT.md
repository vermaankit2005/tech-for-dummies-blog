# About

Most tech writing fails one of two audiences. It either talks down to non-technical readers, or it glosses over the details engineers actually care about.

tech-for-dummies tries to do both at once.

Every post is written in second person — you are the protagonist. You press Enter. You wait. You watch the page appear. The technical explanation is the plot. The jargon is earned, not sprinkled. The numbers are real.

A non-technical reader follows the story and feels smart by the end. An engineer reads the same post and finds something they didn't expect — an exact latency, a protocol name, a design decision they'd never thought to question.

Same post. Same words. Two layers.

## The voice

- Second person, present tense
- Reader acts; devices are extensions of the reader ("your browser" not "the browser")
- Every technical term explained inline, the first time it appears
- Exact numbers over vague impressions
- No "In this post..." openers. No summary at the end. Drop in mid-scene, leave when the story is done.

## The format

Posts are written in HTML with a cream-paper aesthetic. No ads, no popups, no tracking.
